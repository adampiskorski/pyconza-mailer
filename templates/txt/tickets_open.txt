## Tickets

We're excited to announce that early bird tickets for PyConZA 2026 are now on sale!

This year's conference runs from **14–18 October 2026** at J&J Conferences at Belmont Square in Rondebosch, Cape Town, and we have something for everyone:

- **Beginner's Day** (14 Oct): A full-day introduction to Python for first-timers. No prior experience required. Bring a laptop. RSVP is essential as spaces are limited, and this is a **Separate ticket**, which is not for sale yet.</span>
- **Main Conference** (15–16 Oct): Two days of keynotes, talks across three tracks, open spaces, and lightning talks to close.
- **Sprints** (17–18 Oct): Two free days of open-source collaboration. Bring a project, join one, or pair with a maintainer. All welcome.

Can't make it to Cape Town? The main conference will be streamed online and remote tickets are available too.

**Early bird pricing ends midnight on 31 July**, so grab your ticket soon:

👉 https://za.pycon.org/pages/tickets/buy-tickets/

## **Present at PyConZA**

Have something worth sharing with the Python community? We're still accepting talk submissions. We have tracks covering Data Science, Web, Scientific Computing, Testing, Teaching and Learning with Python, and more.

Submit your proposal at https://za.pycon.org/pages/speaking/how_to_apply/

Submission deadline is **31 July 2026**.

First-time speaker? We offer a mentorship programme! Simply email team@za.pycon.org with a rough draft and we'll match you with an experienced speaker.
